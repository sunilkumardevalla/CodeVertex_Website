What the portal is used for

The client portal reduces friction between sessions while maintaining strong privacy standards. It is the preferred channel for non-urgent coordination.

  • Appointment updates and scheduling requests
  • Policy-aware booking changes for paid and free sessions
  • Administrative coordination and next-step clarity

Portal best practices

Use strong passwords, avoid shared devices, and log out after each session when accessing the portal on public or non-private networks.

Keep your booking reference and confirmation email available. These two fields are required for self-service scheduling actions.

Secure Access

Use strong passwords and private devices whenever possible to reduce account risk.

Scheduling Control

Find bookings by reference and email, then reschedule or cancel using policy-aligned actions.

Privacy Discipline

Use portal and forms for scheduling/admin communication, not sensitive diagnostic details.
