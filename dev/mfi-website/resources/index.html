Resources

Trusted tools and references for mental and family wellness

A curated resource library for clients, caregivers, and families seeking evidence-based support information.

How this library supports therapy

Resources can improve continuity between sessions by giving clients reliable frameworks for reflection, skill practice, and informed decision-making.

We recommend using resources as conversation starters in therapy, not as replacements for individualized care planning.

  • Use as educational support between sessions
  • Bring questions from readings into therapy discussions
  • Use crisis and support links for immediate guidance needs

How to get the most value

Pick one or two high-quality resources at a time, apply the ideas during the week, and discuss outcomes in session. Consistent application is more effective than consuming many resources without integration.

How to choose the right resource

Start with the resource category that matches your most immediate concern: relationship communication, emotional regulation, family structure, or crisis support.

If you are unsure which references apply to your case, ask your clinician during session and we will map recommendations to your treatment goals.

Content quality standards

  • Prioritize licensed professional organizations and peer-reviewed references
  • Avoid advice that encourages isolation, shame, or unsafe behavior
  • Use crisis services immediately for urgent safety concerns
